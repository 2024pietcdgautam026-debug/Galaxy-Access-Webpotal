The Sun Contains 99.86% of the Solar System’s Mass

Category: Solar Physics

The Sun holds 99.86% of all the mass in our solar system. This immense gravitational force pulls planets, comets, and asteroids into stable orbits.

The Sun's mass is approximately 1.989 × 10³⁰ kg—over 333,000 times the mass of Earth. This makes it the dominant object in the solar system.

Its energy powers climate, weather, and almost all life processes on Earth.
