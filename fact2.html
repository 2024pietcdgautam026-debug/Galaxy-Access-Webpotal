One Day on Venus Is Longer Than a Year

Category: Planetary Science

Venus has one of the most unusual time cycles in our solar system. A single rotation on its axis takes longer than its orbit around the Sun.

One day on Venus lasts about 243 Earth days, while one year on Venus is only about 225 Earth days. This happens because Venus rotates extremely slowly compared to other planets.

Venus also rotates in the opposite direction to most planets, meaning the Sun rises in the west and sets in the east.
