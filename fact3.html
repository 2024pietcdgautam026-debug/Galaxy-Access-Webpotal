A Neutron Star Is So Dense a Sugar Cube Would Weigh a Billion Tons

Category: Stellar Physics

Neutron stars are formed when massive stars explode in supernovae and their cores collapse under intense gravity.

These stars pack more mass than the Sun into a sphere roughly 20 kilometers wide. Because of this extreme density, a sugar-cube-sized piece of neutron star material would weigh about one billion tons on Earth.

Studying neutron stars helps scientists understand matter under extreme pressure and test the laws of physics.
